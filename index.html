AC Installation — Frequently Asked Questions

Most residential AC installations in the Mill Creek area range from a few thousand dollars for a standard system to more for larger or complex setups. The best way to get an accurate number is a free in-home estimate from a local pro.
Most standard residential AC installations are completed in a single day — typically 4 to 8 hours. More complex jobs involving ductwork or electrical upgrades may take longer, and your technician will give you a realistic timeframe upfront.

AC sizing depends on your home's square footage, insulation, ceiling height, and sun exposure. A technician will perform a load calculation during the estimate to recommend the right unit — oversized or undersized systems both cause problems.
Most HVAC professionals work with several major brands including Carrier, Trane, Lennox, and Rheem. Your technician can walk you through the options that best fit your home and budget.
Most manufacturers recommend a professional tune-up once a year — ideally in spring before cooling season. Regular maintenance extends the life of the system and keeps it running efficiently.

A heat pump both cools and heats your home, making it a year-round solution. A central AC unit only cools — you'd still need a separate heating system. In the Pacific Northwest climate, heat pumps are increasingly popular for their efficiency.
Most AC installations in Washington State require a permit, which your licensed contractor will typically pull on your behalf. This ensures the work is inspected and up to code.
